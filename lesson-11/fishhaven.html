Oneida Stake Academy

by Tadeo Santana

The Oneida Stake Academy was a secondary school operated by the Oneida Stake of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1888 to 1922. The academy building was constructed in Preston, Idaho, in 1895, after the stake headquarters moved from Franklin, Idaho, in 1889.

Among its alumni were Ezra Taft Benson and Harold B. Lee, both of whom were later presidents of the church. Medal of Honor recipients Mervyn S. Bennion, Leonard Brostrom and Junior Van Noy also attended the school.

Following the emergence of the LDS seminaries and better-equipped public schools, the The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints decided to close its system of secondary academies. In 1922, the Oneida Stake Academy was dissolved, although the public school system continued to use the building until 1990.
