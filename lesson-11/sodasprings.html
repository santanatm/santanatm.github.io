Hooper Springs Park

by Tadeo Santana

Hooper Springs is a great place to relax and have a picnic. This is a fun place for children and adults alike. The park has covered pavilions, a basketball court, lots of green grass, picnic benches, and some BBQ pits.

The actual springs are located under a red gazebo near the parking lot. You aren’t allowed to swim in the springs, only drink from it. Just bring your own cup and dip it in the water and give it a taste. Some people really like this stuff and others think it tastes horrible.

The kiosk by the spring will tell you that some of the earliest mentions of this spring come from the 1874 as people were using the Oregon Trail. It hit real fame when ZCMI (Zion’s Cooperative Mercantile Institution) president, W.H. Hooper, built a summer home at the park. He did lots to make the springs known to the world. Eventually a bottling company bottled the soda water and shipped it all over the world. In 1936 improvements were made around the spring to protect it.
